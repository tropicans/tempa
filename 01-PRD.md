# TEMPA Product Requirement Document

## 1. Product Overview

TEMPA (Transformasi Evidence-Based melalui Proyek dan AI) is a project-based learning platform for civil service capability development. The platform embeds AI across the ADDIE cycle to help participants solve real organizational problems, produce measurable implementation outcomes, and generate evidence of competency growth.

TEMPA is designed as a decision-augmentation system. Human users remain accountable for problem selection, solution choice, implementation decisions, and final evaluation.

## 2. Problem Statement

Current civil service learning models are still limited by several issues:

- learning is overly centered on classroom delivery and certification,
- training is weakly connected to strategic organizational problems,
- personalization is limited,
- AI is not yet integrated into the learning design and evaluation flow,
- competency growth is difficult to measure using real implementation evidence.

TEMPA addresses these issues by combining project-based learning, AI assistance, mentor validation, and evidence-based scoring.

## 3. Product Vision

Transform training from a class-based model into an AI-augmented organizational problem-solving laboratory that generates measurable learning, implementation impact, and talent intelligence.

## 4. Product Goals

### Primary Goals

1. Connect learning activities to real organizational challenges.
2. Improve participant decision quality through structured AI assistance.
3. Produce organizational interventions with measurable outcomes.
4. Measure participant growth using evidence-based indicators.
5. Build a reusable talent intelligence foundation for institutions.

### Product Success Metrics

#### Outcome Metrics
- percentage of projects that reach real implementation,
- organizational KPI improvement linked to project execution,
- average and distribution of TAI scores,
- adoption rate of project solutions by institutions.

#### Product Metrics
- participant activation rate,
- completion rate per ADDIE phase,
- mentor review turnaround time,
- AI assist usage rate,
- AI output acceptance vs revision rate,
- project completion rate.

## 5. Target Users

### Primary Users
- Participants in civil service learning programs.

### Secondary Users
- Mentors or coaches who validate analysis, design, and evaluation.

### Operational Users
- Program administrators who manage programs, cohorts, rubrics, and reporting.

### Strategic Stakeholders
- Training institutions,
- ministry or local government leadership,
- talent management or HR stakeholders.

## 6. Product Principles

- Human in the loop: AI recommends, humans decide.
- Evidence-based learning: every phase produces traceable artifacts and metrics.
- Adaptive support: recommendations should adapt to project context and participant needs.
- Explainable AI: outputs must be auditable and grounded.
- Governance by design: security, access control, audit, and scoring traceability are core requirements.

## 7. In Scope

### MVP Scope
- SSO and user onboarding,
- program and cohort management,
- participant project workspace,
- problem statement submission,
- AI-assisted problem analysis,
- AI-assisted decision option generation,
- implementation plan drafting,
- reflection journal submission,
- basic TAI scoring,
- participant and mentor dashboards,
- approval workflow for mentor checkpoints,
- audit logs for critical actions.

### Out Of Scope For MVP
- national-scale multi-tenant federation,
- advanced predictive analytics,
- cross-project intelligence and knowledge graph,
- executive benchmarking across institutions,
- full enterprise data warehouse optimization,
- sophisticated recommendation engines beyond core AI assistance.

## 8. High-Level User Journey

1. Participant joins a program and creates a project.
2. Participant submits a problem statement.
3. AI assists with reframing, root cause analysis, and stakeholder mapping.
4. Mentor validates the problem definition.
5. Participant generates and reviews solution options.
6. Participant selects a preferred direction and creates an implementation plan.
7. Mentor validates the implementation blueprint.
8. Participant records progress and uploads impact evidence.
9. Participant submits a reflection journal.
10. System calculates TAI and displays progress on the dashboard.

## 9. MVP Value Proposition

The MVP should prove three things before broader enterprise expansion:

1. participants can complete a guided project workflow,
2. mentors can review and validate key checkpoints efficiently,
3. AI assistance improves speed and structure without removing human accountability.

## 10. Highest-Priority Product Improvements

1. Freeze MVP scope and stop mixing MVP with national-scale future state.
2. Define a canonical workflow state model for all ADDIE phases.
3. Turn each feature list into testable acceptance criteria.
4. Finalize a single role model and permission matrix.
5. Define product analytics and operational metrics from day one.
